There are multiple options for lodging in Ithaca, NY. The links will lead you to the respective on- and off-campus lodging options.

  • Statler Hotel is located in the heart of Cornell’s campus, the Statler is an innovative leader in hospitality.
  • Best Western University Inn is located in the heart of downtown along the Ithaca Commons, enjoy access to restaurants, shops, and entertainment. Ride TCAT Bus #10 from the Seneca Street Parking Garage for easy access to campus.
  • Hilton Garden Inn is located in downtown Ithaca, opposite restaurants and bars at Ithaca Commons. The campuses of Ithaca College and Cornell University are both within a mile. We offer free WiFi, an indoor pool, and a 24-hour fitness center. For your convenience, The Shop 24 offers drinks and snacks 24 hours a day.
  • Hotel Ithaca is located in downtown Ithaca. As the area's only independently owned hotel, the hotel provides a unique blend of comfort and convenience for visitors to this picturesque Finger Lakes town.
  • The Argos Inn is located in downtown Ithaca. A beautifully restored, LEED certified, historic mansion in the heart of Ithaca, NY. Formerly the Mayor's house and the Duncan Hines world headquarters.
  • Ithaca Marriott is situated in the heart of Downtown Ithaca Commons, moments away from Cornell University and Ithaca College, our hotel invites you to embrace the season's vibrancy. Wander along restaurant row, explore local galleries, and indulge in boutique shopping—all just a stroll away.

Workshop participants can take the TCAT bus to the Colloquium venue.

  • Mobile bus passes purchased and used through the Tfare app (available on the Apple App Store for iOS and the Google Play store).
  • Period passes are day-based passes good for unlimited rides during a period of consecutive days. Passes purchased are activated after the first use, and the fare box will validate the pass automatically. Frequent riders can enjoy bigger discounts by buying a weekly, monthly, or annual pass. Period passes are also available in 1-Day, 2-Day, and 5-Day increments. Please note that a “Day” is a Service Day, NOT a 24 hour period. A service day begins when the first bus leaves the garage in the morning (~4:00am) and ends when the last bus returns at night (~2:30am).
